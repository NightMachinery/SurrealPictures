Vertical 2:3 frame, a full-body mythic figure poised on a suspended “vine elevator” platform inside a cramped jungle ravine that feels like a throat of greenery and stone: an architect-beloved with an unreadable face (half serene, half exhausted), bare feet planted, torso partly draped in torn, ceremonial fabric and dangling measuring cords; one hand cradles a delicate, intimate gesture (fingers touching their own sternum as if checking a heartbeat), the other hand extends outward as if releasing something they’ve carried too long. The ravine is an ornamental labyrinth made of interlocked arches, carved roots, and filigreed lianas forming frames-within-frames around the body; every surface is crowded with reliefs that resemble floorplans, ribcages, and floral patterns at once, so the environment can be read as architecture or anatomy. Grotesque/uncanny details everywhere: vines that end in fingertip tendrils, orchids with ear-like petals, stone openings shaped like soft mouths, tiny elevator platforms that look like beds, confessionals, or coffins; several masked silhouettes hide in the greenery and rock apertures, not clearly human, not clearly statues. The atmosphere is palpable relief (weight lifted): storm-dark greens and deep shadow in the ravine’s recesses, but a warm, post-rain amber light breaks in from above, illuminating the figure’s shoulders and the hovering architecture as if gravity has softened; mist curls like a slow exhale around the ankles, and the entire crowded scene feels one breath after survival—tender, uneasy, and impossible, with dense detail in all corners and no empty horizon.
