a cradle is present not as furniture but as architecture: a gilt cradle-frame fused into the stone floor like a ceremonial trapdoor, its curved rails grown into the room’s ribs, rocking without moving, lined with immaculate white cloth that glows as if it remembers daylight; from the cradle erupts a beanstalk made of impossible materials—braided museum labels, pearl threads, vine, and braided hair—climbing straight up through locked ceiling beams, yet its leaves are porcelain replicas of classical hands and feet, each leaf delicately cracked and still warm;
