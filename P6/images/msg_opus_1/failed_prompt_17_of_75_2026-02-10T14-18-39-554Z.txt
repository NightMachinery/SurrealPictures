The primary artistic tradition is Gustav Klimt's gilded Art Nouveau world --- iridescent gold leaf, jewel-tone palettes of sapphire, ruby, emerald, and amethyst, organic spiraling flourishes, and a devotion to decorative saturation that borders on the sacred. Recall how Klimt's figures emerge from golden pattern-fields in states of undress, their nakedness inseparable from the ornamentation surrounding them. That fusion is your model: the human body treated the way Klimt, Schiele, Mucha, and the great classical painters treated it --- as a site of vulnerability, desire, power, and unresolved contradiction, never as mere decoration. A nude or partially nude figure entangled with architecture, consumed by pattern, dissolving into landscape, or simply existing in raw unguarded stillness within a fever-dream setting --- these collisions between flesh and fantasy are where the most potent ambiguity lives. Built on this Klimt foundation is a specific rendering technique applied as a global rule. Every visible surface without exception --- skin, hair, clothing if any, architecture, sky, water, stone --- must be filled with a rich intricate pattern texture: brocade, damask, circuitry, thorn filigree, stained glass, woodgrain, starfields, lacework. On bare skin this effect is especially striking: the nude body becomes a living tapestry, flesh and ornament fused into something hovering in an uncanny space between icon and organism. Crucially, these pattern layers must remain perfectly flat and static, like a collage cutout or a window into a separate decorative dimension. They never warp, bend, stretch, or follow the three-dimensional curvature of the forms they cover. The 3D shapes underneath may twist, fold, and recede freely, but the pattern skins stay locked and unmoving --- a hypnotic visual paradox between flat ornament and volumetric form. This is not a selective flourish; it is the fundamental visual logic of the entire image.

The composition must be maximalist and claustrophobic in its richness. The mood is a frozen dream: one impossible instant suspended at the precise tipping point between two irreconcilable states --- beauty and ruin, intimacy and vastness, tenderness and threat, the sacred and the grotesque.
