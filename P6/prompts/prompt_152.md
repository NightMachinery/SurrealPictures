Over-the-shoulder viewpoint from behind **The Host**: a mythic, androgynous figure seated sideways on a suspended stair-landing throne, spine tall yet tender, one bare shoulder and back exposed like a classical statue under ceremonial regalia—half tailcoat, half cassock—whose surfaces are not fabric but **flat, unmoving 2D screen-tone overlays** of **floral chintz, bone marbling, worn leather grain, and translucent amber** that stubbornly stay planar as the body turns beneath them (collage paradox, pattern does *not* follow folds). The district is an **endless staircase city with no ground level**, packed wall-to-wall with landings, balconies, banisters, doors that open onto more stairs, and staircases that braid into knots; every surface is crowded with **hairline cracks** like delicate lightning, and **porcelain shards** drift in suspended spirals, catching blinding celebratory light like confetti. The impossible center of the frozen dream is a brutal, unmistakable **flora/fauna fusion**: topiary animals bloom into living bouquets—stags whose antlers are flowering hedgerows, koi whose scales are rose petals, owls with orchid faces—while vines grow feathered wings and birds molt into leaves; a lion made of peonies exhales moths, and a cypress-bodied horse sheds butterflies that **migrate** in organized chaos through the stair canyons, filling every gap of air. The atmosphere is **euphoria—blinding joy**: radiant jewel-toned glare, ecstatic halos, gold-reflecting dust, overexposed highlights that feel like laughter made visible; yet the Host’s posture holds solemn hospitality, as if welcoming the viewer into a celebration that might also be a trap. In the corners and midground, the “guests” of the cycle—faceless courtiers, porcelain-masked servants, flower-beasts—perform a **tea-party etiquette** that has spiraled into absurd procedure: impossibly long teaspoons, bows measured with protractors, a tiny rulebook chained to a saucer, napkins folded into labyrinth maps, a clockwork teapot pouring into cups that are already full, all subtle and half-hidden among patterned rugs and shelf-trinkets.
