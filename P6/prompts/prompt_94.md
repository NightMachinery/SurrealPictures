Snowglobes and mini dioramas pile along the lip of the stage and float at shoulder height: tiny versions of the fossil forest amphitheater containing tiny hourglasses containing tinier voids; a few globes are cracked, their artificial snow escaping as glittering flecks that become real constellations across the frame. The Matchmaker’s expression is victorious elation with a bruise of empathy—eyes bright, mouth almost smiling, as if they have finally orchestrated a union between two impossible things: presence and absence; their skin and garments shimmer with iridescent gold-leaf filigree and jeweled ornament, yet the void behind them drinks color into matte black.
