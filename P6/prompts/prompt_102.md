Their hands cradle **a shared cup** held between them and their own reflection, but the cup is paradoxically *one object in two places*: in the left panel it is pressed to their sternum, in the right panel it floats a handspan away, as if forgiveness has loosened gravity’s claim; the liquid inside is not wine but luminous spores and small, forgiven memories (tiny stamped letters, melted wax, petal-like fungi) suspended mid-splash—everything frozen at peak euphoria, blinding joy pouring outward like light.
