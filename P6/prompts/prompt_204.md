Their posture is mid-curtsy, mid-lunge—one hand offering a jeweled domino to the viewer, the other palming a stolen heirloom heart—smiling with triumph that is also grief. Physics is loudly wrong: **solid liquidity** dominates the scene as basalt and marble flow like water yet hold their shape—frozen mid-surge in impossible curls, a river of stone pouring upward into chandeliers of fossil sap, droplets of granite suspended like pearls around the Trickster’s ankles; a “splash” of obsidian behaves like silk, while a ribbon of “water” lands with the weight of stone. The very inheritance of the place becomes visible: ancestral faces trapped in ambered bark lean out of the seats, passing keys mouth-to-mouth; roots thread through ribs of architecture like genealogical veins, each branch terminating in a lock that fits no door.
