A vertical 2:3 gallery illustration framed like a crowded Dixit card: inside an opulent bathhouse choked with rose steam and echoing porcelain murals, a mythic athlete (androgynous, classical musculature, damp skin catching gold light; half-draped in a torn ceremonial starting-bib that reads like a relic) is frozen mid‑stride on the lip of a pool—yet the same body is duplicated in a cascading time-collapse: one version launching, one suspended, one already landing, one kneeling in breathless awe, overlapping like misregistered animation cels; their shadows disagree, splitting into different directions as if time has cracked. Beneath them, the bathwater is not water but a perfectly matte void: a starless, depthless cutout shaped like an impossible lane-marker that swallows reflections while still holding ripples in place; porcelain tiles around it pucker inward, murals of cherubs and athletes flake into the darkness, and the architecture seems to both birth and consume the runner. The “Gankutsuou effect” is unmistakable: all figures, columns, and steam are overlaid with flat, unmoving 2D screen-tone collage patterns of gemstones and rust—iridescent jewel facets and corroded iron filigree sliding across limbs and arches without bending to anatomy or folds, as if the world is wearing a static tapestry while moving underneath. In the athlete’s outstretched hand, a warm-luminous golden egg glows like a private sunrise, casting childlike wonder into their widened eyes; lodged in the mural behind their heartline, a half-burned candle persists as a totem, its flame paused mid-flicker while thick wax drips hang in midair like stalactites, each drip containing tiny trapped reflections of different moments of the same athlete. No negative space: the foreground is a riot of scattered marbles, glass beads, prism drops, salt crystals, and sugar dust clinging to wet tiles and floating through steam like slow snowfall; midground strands of wax thread between suspended droplets; background alcoves teem with ornate faucets shaped like mythic mouths exhaling rose vapor, porcelain saints with cracked glazed smiles, and mural panels that repeat the athlete in contradictory eras—child, adult, elder—stacked in one impossible frame. Lighting is soft-gilded and exploratory (the mood of first discovery), with jewel-tone highlights bouncing off beads and gold leaf glints in the murals, while the void remains perfectly unlit, a silent riddle at the center of all motion, pulling every object into a delicate, frozen orbit.
