A colossal elder-giant stands at a crowded border checkpoint where spring and winter collide like incompatible paperwork: one half of the gate is a thawing greenhouse of feverish blossoms, hummingbird-moths, and sweating fruit; the other half is a customs hall carved from blue ice, brittle firs, and snow that falls upward in neat columns like stamped forms. The giant’s posture is both guardian and prisoner—knees bent as if mid-step yet forever paused, one hand bracing an ornate turnstile shaped like a spiral maze, the other holding a conch-shell “passport” that unfurls into a **tidal wave** frozen mid-crash: a towering crest of translucent water that has become architecture, curling into labyrinthine corridors filled with faces, tiny doors, stairways, and contradictory signs pointing everywhere at once. Dream logic erupts: koi fish bloom with peony scales; foxes wear orchid wings; antlers sprout fern-fronds that end in staring eyes; swans hatch from seedpods; a stag’s breath becomes a bouquet of beetles; flora and fauna fuse into living contraband being gently searched by blindfolded cherubs made of seaweed and lace. The frame is packed—foreground customs counters piled with domino tiles, dice, game tokens and broken compasses; midair drifts with lanterns that float like captured suns; butterfly pins and jeweled brooches orbit like tiny planets, pinning shadows to walls, pinning memories to skin, pinning time to the wave’s rim. In the corners, queues of miniature travelers loop into impossible Escher lines through the wave-maze, exchanging heart-shaped stamps, trading winter gloves for flower petals; a naked marble torso on a pedestal (sensual but vulnerable) is being gently dressed in snowfall by spring vines, while a coat of blossoms is being buttoned shut by frost. Everything is euphoric—blinding joy as a physical force: prismatic gold light floods the scene, making tears look like glittering saltwater pearls, turning every crack into a smile and every barrier into a celebration; yet the checkpoint remains unreadable, a riddle that feels like triumph.
