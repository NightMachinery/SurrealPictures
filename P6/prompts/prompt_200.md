The environment reacts to the act of giving: hairline cracks in the basalt glow from within like kintsugi veins, repairing themselves where the red threads touch; porcelain shards float around the offering hand in a paused orbit, their edges reflecting aurora greens; ripe fruit and split pomegranates and dark berries tumble through the air without falling, seeds suspended like tiny hearts, some lodged gently in the cracks as if planted.
