A frozen dream-frame inside a baroque observatory-catholic nave hybrid where the floorplan is a star map: the Matchmaker–Cartographer stands center-left on a cracked compass-rose dais, one bare shoulder and ribcage exposed like a classical statue unfinished, skin inscribed with delicate longitude lines and love-letters in dead languages; they lean forward as if offering a ring, yet their other hand pins a parchment atlas to the air itself with a silver needle, posture both tender and exhausted, eyes fixed on something that is not there. The defining impossibility: an **invisible presence** occupies the scene like a missing monument—chairs are indented by unseen weight, a veil is being lifted by absent fingers, salt-sugar dust blossoms outward from a handprint that has no hand, porcelain shards hover in a perfect spiral as if caught in the wake of a body you cannot see; a wine-dark ribbon of “matchmaking thread” is pulled taut into a kiss-shape around empty space, knotting itself to nothing, while the protagonist’s lips tremble a millimeter from an unseen cheek. In the heart of the cathedral-observatory yawns the theme made physical: a **mystery/void**—not a hole but a meticulous absence, a black ellipse rimmed with hairline cracks that branch like constellations across pillars, swallowing reflected faces from dozens of floating **empty frames** that try to depict what refuses to appear; each frame shows a different near-truth (a silhouette, a map, a pair of hands) but the center is always erased. The aurora borealis is not in the sky but **woven into architecture**: a luminous curtain of green-violet aurora hangs like stained-glass drapery behind the subject, threaded through chandeliers and sewn into their cloak-lining and the atlas margins, casting longing-colored light (aching distance, late-night gold and bruised teal) that makes every object look like it is remembering someone far away. Crowd every corner with evidence and contradiction: foreground—spilled cartographer tools, compass needles, wax seals, shards of porcelain teacups frozen mid-fall, sugar crystals glittering on eyelashes, mirror splinters reflecting an absent body; midground—spiraling frames, map scrolls unrolling into the void, cracked marble steps with tiny footprints that stop abruptly; background—ornate arches packed with Klimt-like mosaics and occult coordinates, a telescope aimed at nothing, balconies lined with faceless mannequins in formalwear holding bouquets for a bride who never arrives. Apply the Gankutsuou collage rule aggressively: all 3D forms (skin, cloth, stone, frames, debris, even the void’s rim) are covered in **flat, unmoving 2D screen-tone overlays** of **sand grain, mirror tessellations, stained-glass florals, and magma marbling** that do not follow folds or perspective—patterns slide like paper cutouts over living bodies, creating a luxurious wrongness; the aurora light catches the gold leaf and jewel tones, making the scene opulent yet wounded, sensual but unreachable, every object pressed close with no empty space, a visual riddle about who is being matched to whom when the most important figure cannot be seen.
