Portrait-format, gallery-quality surrealist illustration: from a **behind-the-curtain vantage**, the viewer peers through layers of **translucent, wind-tugged textiles** (gauze, organza, and a torn **Derafsh Kaviani** standard repurposed as a veil), their weave catching meteor-dust like constellations; beyond the fabric, a **meteor-glass quarry** crowds the frame—black iridescent obsidian cliffs, prismatic slag heaps, suspended shards, ladders, pulleys, and delicate workbenches—yet everything is frozen mid-catastrophe as if the dream has paused on the exact inhale before impact. In the center stands the mythic protagonist: **a musician entwined with two lovers as one triune figure**, timeless and vulnerable—bare-backed classical nudity partially wrapped in the banner-cloth, gold-thread scars like musical staves across skin, one lover’s hand over the musician’s sternum, the other lover reaching away, faces turned in opposite directions with the same expression of **longing at an aching distance**; the musician’s posture is reverent and strained, chin lifted as if listening for an answer the quarry refuses to give. The **Eureka / discovery** moment manifests physically: the musician has just plucked a single string stretched across the quarry, and the **singing winds** are visible as translucent ribbons of air shaped into notation, spiraling into a sudden, impossible **lightbulb-shaped void** carved out of the dust—inside it, a map of hidden veins in the meteor glass glows like a living brain. Make the broken logic blatant with **scale inversion**: a **tiny Sphinx** (no larger than a fist) sits on the musician’s shoulder like a pet, yet it casts the shadow of a **colossal Sphinx** that is nowhere to be seen; meanwhile, the quarry’s cranes and workers are **miniature**, delicately chiseling a **mountain-sized tuning fork**, and a single tear suspended on the lover’s cheek is **as large as a boulder**, reflecting the entire scene like a lens. Fill every corner with riddling details—floating tools, cracked mirrors, moths made of glass filings, caged wind-chimes, broken amphorae pouring sand that hangs motionless, lovers’ fingerprints visible in dust on crystal surfaces; in the far cluttered background, the Derafsh’s jeweled center becomes a makeshift altar, and the quarry walls are carved with riddle-glyphs that almost form faces. Hide a subtle homage: a **space ranger helmet** as a small overlooked trinket—half-buried in glass grit on a shelf near the edge, or reflected faintly in a shard near the curtain—never central.
